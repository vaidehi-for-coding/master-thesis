<FILTER> Bubble

A master thesis user study

The term filter bubble was first used by internet activist Eli Pariser in his book, "The Filter Bubble: What the Internet Is Hiding from You". He performed a simple experiment - he asked two of his friends to google for the word 'Egypt' and asked them to send him screenshots of their search results. One friend received search results regarding protests and crises in Egypt, while the other friend received travel information and general news of Egypt.

Filter Bubble

Since recommendation systems usually rely on your internet history and other user histories similar to yours to recommend items, due to eventual overspecialization of the algorithm you are exposed to information that the algorithm thinks you want to see, and not necessarily what you need to see. This ends up creating a filter bubble (also known as an information bubble/echo chamber) where you are no longer exposed to diverse themes and opinions, instead you end up consuming the same kind of content again and again.
